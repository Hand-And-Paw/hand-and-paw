Why adoption is important?

You give the animal a better life
All homeless animals have been lost, given up or abandoned. You are giving them a new life in a loving home.

You help break the cycle of pet overpopulation
There are not enough homes for all the animals that are born every year. You confront irresponsible owners who don't sterilize or neuter their pets.

If you adopt, you won't be supporting puppy mills and pet stores
Most animals raised for sale are housed in poor conditions: in cages and without human companionship. Puppy mills will continue to operate until people stop purchasing from them.

You encourage others to adopt animals
When your friends ask where you got your amazing pet, you can tell them “He is adopted.” Your happy companionship will help to break stereotype that you can't bond with adopted animal.

When a lonely animal needs our help?

The main reasons animals are in shelters: owners give them up, owners passed away or somebody finds them on the street.

Many strays are lost pets that were not kept properly indoors or provided with identification.

Don't be afraid of the stray animal and try to help them to find a new home, avoiding stress of being in overcrowded shelter or being euthanized.
