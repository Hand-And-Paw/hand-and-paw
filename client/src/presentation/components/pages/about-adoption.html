Frequently asked questions.

  • Where can I adopt a pet in Belgium?
    • You can use shelter search or pet search on our website.
    • You can adopt laboratory animal(it is not only rats, but cats, dogs(often beagles), chikens, fish and etc) for free. In Belgium you can contact vzw Jodipro or ask for more information from Adoption Commission of UZ Ghent - adoptiedgk@ugent.be.
    • You can symbolically adopt a wild animal in Zonnegloed vzw sanctuary. The animal will still live in the Sanctuary, but you will be getting updates about it.
  • Which animals can I have as a pet in Belgium?
    • Cats and Dogs.
    • Other mammals.
      Mammals allowed in Flemish region.
      Mammals allowed in Brussels region.
      Mammals allowed in Wallonia region.
    • Birds, except for invasive or protected species. To be sure, you can contact this organization.
    • Reptiles(lizards, snakes, turtles).
      • Reptiles allowed in Flemish region.
      Reptiles allowed in Brussels region. Reptiles allowed in Wallonia region.

      If the animal is not in the list, but is given for adoption you can consult with local Police wether it would be legal to get this animal.

    • Invertebraes
  • What are legal obligations for a pet owner in Belgium?
    • Dogs
      • All dogs must be microchipped and registered before the age of 8 weeks. Th seller must transfer the chip in the name of new owner via www.dogid.be.
      Puppy must stay with the mother for minimum 8 weeks.
      If puppy comes from abroad, it must be vaccinated against rabies.
    • Cats
      • All cats must be microchipped and registered before the age of 12 weeks. Th seller must transfer the chip in the name of new owner via www.catid.be.
      All cats(exception for catteries) must be neutered before they are sold or adopted before 5 months old.
  • Am I ready for a pet?
    Ask yourself these questions:
    • does you rent contract allow you to have pets and which?
    • do you have enough space and time?
    • how much money are you ready to spend monthly on a pet
    • who will take care of the pet every day?
    • who will take care of the pet during vacations? or can you afford pet hotel?
    • does everyone in my family want an animal?
    • am I or someone in my family allergic to...
    • how old and how big should my pet be?
    • will other pets accept a new friend?
  • Dogs are the cutest animals on Earth. What should I take in consideration if I want a dog?
    Ask yourself these questions:
    • do I have space and time to play on daily basis, ideally outside for the dog?
    • Do I have enough enough space inside the house?
    • Am I ready t walk with the dog even if it is raining or cold?
    • Am I ready to clean the dog poo?(some dogs can get pot training, but you still will have to deal with it especially outside)
    • A dog may cost you 700-1300 per year + unforeseen spending for medical care. Am I financially ready for that?
    • Am I ready to take care of a dog for next 10-20 years?
    • Do I have a solution for dog care if I go on vacations without my dog?
    • Do my family members share your passion for dogs?
    • Do I or them have pet allergies?
    • Am I ready for happy but very dirty dog?
    • Am I ready that dog may break things?
    • Does the dog I visually like has character I like and ready to deal with?

    If you are not answering yes to all those questions, it doesn't mean you can't have a dog. How dog behaves and what it needs varies. It depends on it's breed, upbringing, it's mental and health condition. Care takers in the shelters will be glad to answer your questions and tell you all they know about taking care of dogs in general and will share what they know about the dog you like in their shelter. In some shelters you can become a volunteer,take dog for some time or get familiar with dog you like by walking with it.

  • Cats are the cutest animals on Earth. What should I take in consideration if I want a dog?
    Ask yourself these questions:
    • Will I still love it if it doesn't like to cuddle, lie on my lap and as not much interest in me?
    • Am I ready for mices and birds it may bring home?
    • Am I ready to take care of a cat for next 15-20 years?
    • Do I have a solution for cat care if I go on vacations without my cat?
    • Do my family members share your passion for cats?
    • Do I or them have pet allergies?
    • A cat will cost you minimum 400 euro per year + unforeseen spending for medical care. Am I financially ready for that?
    • Do I have space for litter box abd scratching posts in the house?
    • Am I ready to give my cat freedom to go where she wants when I am not at home or it needs to be limited? How is it possible?

    Cat's are a bit more tricky than dogs, but there is still room for choice. Cats have different characters like any other animals and if you are looking for specific personality, care taker in a shelter will advise you about their cats. Shelters often seek for temporary family for their cats and kittens - this may help you to find your perfect companion.
